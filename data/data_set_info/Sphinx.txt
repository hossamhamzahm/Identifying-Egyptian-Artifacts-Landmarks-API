The Sphinx, a mythical creature with a lion's body and a human head, holds significance in Egyptian and Greek art and legend. The famed winged Sphinx of Thebes terrorized people with a riddle, eventually solved by Oedipus. The Great Sphinx at Giza, a colossal recumbent statue, dates back to King Khafre. The Sphinx's portrayal varied in Asia and Greece, evolving from the Bronze Age to the Classical period. Sphinxes adorned vases, ivories, and temples, reflecting protective symbolism. In Greek literature, Oedipus' encounter with the Sphinx gained prominence, transitioning from a physical to a mental challenge, illustrating the Sphinx's enduring cultural influence.
