Khan El Khalili, Africa's oldest and largest bazaar, situated in Islamic Cairo, boasts a history dating back to the late 14th century. Spanning over 5,000 square meters, it has become a vital hub for Cairenes, offering a lively space for socializing, shopping, and exchanging news. Drawing 3,000 visitors daily, it has inspired Nobel Prize-winning author Naguib Mahfouz and features prominently in films and literature. The market offers diverse goods, including antiques, silverware, gold artifacts, stained-glass lamps, incense, and handcrafted accessories. Dedicated districts cater to gold, copper, and spices, creating a vast and maze-like shopping experience.
